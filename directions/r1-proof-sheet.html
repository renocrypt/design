Most writing on machine learning asks for faith. A result is quoted, a benchmark is waved through, and the reader is left to trust the citation chain. The alternative is slower and rarer: show the derivation, run the experiment in public, and let the reader verify every step.

That is the entire editorial program here. Long-form pieces with the math attached — notation introduced before use, code that runs, and claims scoped to what the evidence carries.
