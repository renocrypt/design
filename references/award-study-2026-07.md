# Award-site study notes — 2026-07-19 (visual, first-hand)

Studied by actually rendering the winners headlessly, not reading summaries.

## Hiroto Sato (SOTD, personal portfolio)
- White page, ONE signature 3D object (traffic signal wearing street signs
  with his name). Scroll drives the camera around the object; the typographic
  chrome stays fixed. Name is small and disciplined; personality lives in the
  object, not the UI.
- Lesson: a site needs a signature object with a concept. Type-only decoration
  reads as template.

## MONOLOG (Typography Honors)
- Mega-wordmark in ghost gray bleeding off the viewport edge — type as
  architecture, not headline. Tiny centered statement floating above it.
- Topographic contour texture instead of plain grain.
- B&W founder photography with giant type overlapping it.
- Lesson: extreme scale contrast (12px vs 500px) + a motif texture.

## House of Honey
- Bespoke wordmark lockup mixing two type personalities (chunky grotesque +
  calligraphic swash "of").
- Full-bleed lush photography with a paper-cut collage element breaking the
  photo's frame.
- Lesson: lockups should be bespoke; collage (tactile rebellion) beats polish.

## Anti-slop rules confirmed visually
- No dots-and-lines "network graphs" (the #1 AI-slop trope).
- No purple-cyan gradients, glassmorphism, decorative bento.
- No fade-up-on-everything; stage reveals selectively.
- Grain at 3–8% is the anti-AI-polish signal; warm neutrals over pure b/w.
